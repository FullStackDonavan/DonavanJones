## Who This Is For

You're running (or planning to run) a homelab on Raspberry Pi hardware and you'd rather skip the weeks of trial and error that come with figuring out node roles, networking, and storage from scratch. This blueprint is the condensed version of everything documented across the [Infrastructure Engineering series](/categories/infrastructure-engineering) — the actual hardware, manifests, and decisions behind a real rack, not a theoretical one.

## What's Inside

- **Hardware list** — every part used, what it costs, and why it was chosen over the alternatives
- **Network diagram** — how the rack connects to the router, how nodes talk to each other, and where the bottlenecks are
- **Node roles** — which machine runs the control plane, which run workloads, and how to decide that split for your own hardware
- **Folder structures** — how manifests, secrets, and configs are organized so the repo stays navigable as the cluster grows
- **Kubernetes manifests** — working K3s, Prometheus, and Grafana manifests you can adapt directly
- **Troubleshooting checklist** — the failure modes that actually show up on ARM64 hardware, and how to diagnose them fast

## Why It's Different From Just Reading the Blog

The blog series walks through the reasoning behind each decision. This blueprint is the reference you keep open while you're actually building — the parts list, the diagrams, and the manifests in one place, without re-reading 47 articles to find the YAML you need.
